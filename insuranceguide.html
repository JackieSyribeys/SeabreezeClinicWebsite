Your Guide to Dental Insurance

Know your benefits. Choose your dentist with confidence.

Understanding dental insurance can feel overwhelming, but choosing the right plan doesn't have to be. This guide explains how out-of-network benefits work, what to look for in a PPO plan, and the questions you should ask before enrolling.

Not All Dental Insurance Plans Are Created Equal

In today's dental world, insurance plans vary widely, and not all of them work in your favor.

If you want the freedom to choose your own dentist—including specialists or providers you've trusted for years—it's important to understand how out-of-network benefits actually work.

What Is Out-of-Network Coverage?

Out-of-network coverage means you can visit any licensed dentist, even if they don't have a contract with your insurance company.

With the right plan, you'll still receive reimbursement for your care, often at the same rate as in-network providers.

Many patients mistakenly believe they must stay in-network. Depending on your plan, you may have the freedom to choose the dentist you trust without paying significantly more.

Questions to Ask Your Insurance Broker

Coverage & Reimbursement

  • Does this plan pay the same for in-network and out-of-network care?
  • What UCR percentile does the plan use: 80th, 90th, or higher?
  • Is reimbursement based on UCR or a fixed fee schedule?
  • Can I see a sample reimbursement chart for common procedures?

Benefits & Limitations

  • Are there waiting periods for preventative, basic or major services?
  • What is the annual maximum benefit?
  • Are implants, crowns, bridges, or cosmetic procedures covered?
  • Are there any exclusions or limitations?
  • Does this plan have out-of-network benefits?

What to Review Before Enrolling

Summary of Benefits

A clear list of covered procedures so you understand what your plan includes and avoid unexpected costs.

Sample Explanation of Benefits (EOB)

Shows how much the insurance company actually pays for common procedures.

Fee Schedule or UCR Chart

Reveals the dollar amount your plan allows for each procedure.

🚨 Beware of "Too Good to Be True" Plans

  • Low-cost plans often come with hidden limitations.
  • Some plans advertise 80% reimbursement but calculate that percentage using a dramatically reduced allowable amount.
  • Many insurers rely on internal fee schedules or low UCR percentiles that don't reflect what dentists actually charge.
  • You could end up paying hundreds more out-of-pocket than expected.
  • Always confirm whether the plan has waiting periods before certain procedures become eligible for coverage.
